How calibration works:
Every screen has a different pixel density, which means true scale images can only be shown once the pixel-per-inch (PPI) ratio is known. This page will help you quickly calibrate your screen to show the correct scale by calculating that ratio.

You should only have to calibrate this once.

After calibration, your screen will continue to display the scale correctly unless you:

  1. Use a different browser. Since screen calibration relies on browser cookies, only the originally calibrated browser will scale correctly unless you calibrate the new browser as well.
  2. Switch computers. Each computer/laptop screen is different and will have to be calibrated separately.
  3. Clear your browser cookies manually.

Instructions:
To calibrate your screen, pick one of the two calibration measures below.

Method #1:
Hold up a penny to the box on the right and drag that box until it fits the penny perfectly.

Method #2:
Hold up a ruler to the box on the left and drag that box until it matches a width of 1 inch.

Once either of the boxes has been dragged to the correct size, click 'calibrate' below it. The test image beneath will then re-size to test your screen calibration, and that's it!
